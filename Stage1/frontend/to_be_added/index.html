How learning works

Three motions, every module,
every lesson.

A consistent rhythm students internalize after two classes — then carry into every experiment that follows.

1

Encounter

Open with an analogy, a story, or a dilemma. No vocabulary, no jargon — just a hook from a 12-year-old's world.

2

Build

Train a model, wire an agent, or argue a case. Hands on the keyboard within 90 seconds of opening any lesson.

3

Reflect

Explain what happened. Predict what would change. Argue whether it should. Reflection closes every lesson — no exceptions.
